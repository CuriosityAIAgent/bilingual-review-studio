# How to get to 9,000 on the S&P 500

Near all-time highs and a ~16% rally from the March pullback, the S&P 500 has staged a historic rally driven by an earnings supercycle and the pricing in of an artificial intelligence (AI) revolution. While not the base case, the S&P 500 could reach as high as 9,000 by mid-2027. A ~22% gain from current levels may seem optimistic, but remains plausible.

## A bigger, broader AI supercycle

Global earnings growth is booming, accelerating from 15.3% year-over-year in the fourth quarter to 22.6% in the first quarter of this year, the highest in over four years. There has not been a streak of six consecutive quarters of double-digit earnings growth since the aftermath of the global financial crisis.

The largest cloud giants are deploying over $800 billion in AI capital spending annually, scaling to an estimated $1.16 trillion by 2027. This acts as a massive stimulus for the private sector, flowing directly into semiconductors, specialized hardware, advanced cooling, power grids and commercial construction.

For companies to sustain mid-teens earnings growth without igniting inflation, productivity needs to accelerate. Productivity boomed in the late 1990s, annualizing ~2.8%, while the index delivered five consecutive years of over 20% returns between 1995 and 2000.

## Digesting a bond market sell-off

U.S. 10-year yields rose by over 40 basis points over the last four weeks. U.K. gilt yields, German bunds and Japanese government bonds experienced similar moves, driven by a repricing of central bank policy expectations. It is not the level of rates itself, but the magnitude of the move, that matters more. Similar moves have happened six times in the last five years.

## Hormuz hangover

A return to normal around the Strait of Hormuz, which would return over 14 million barrels of oil per day to the global economy, could reverse the effect, at least temporarily. The regions that have suffered most from higher energy costs and a negative terms-of-trade shock, like Europe or Japan, could see a boost.
