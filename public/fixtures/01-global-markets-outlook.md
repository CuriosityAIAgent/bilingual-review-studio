# Global Markets Outlook: Q2 2026

## Executive Summary

The global economy enters the second quarter of 2026 on firmer footing than many had feared a year ago. Growth has proven resilient, inflation has continued its gradual descent toward central bank targets, and the policy backdrop has begun to ease. We expect developed-market central banks to deliver a cumulative 250 basis points of easing over the coming twelve months, though the path will be uneven across regions.

## Macroeconomic Backdrop

Headline inflation across the major economies has settled near 3.5%, still above target but trending lower. Labor markets remain tight by historical standards, which gives central banks reason to move cautiously. We anticipate the first interest rate adjustment in the third quarter, with subsequent moves contingent on incoming wage and services data.

## Asset Allocation Views

Against this backdrop we maintain a modest overweight to equities, funded from cash. Within fixed income we favor investment grade credit over high yield, where spreads have compressed to levels that no longer adequately compensate for default risk. We hold a neutral stance on duration and continue to monitor the shape of the yield curve for signals on the timing of the easing cycle.

## Technology and Data Infrastructure

Our investment process is increasingly data-driven. Each portfolio manager now relies on a dedicated computer running our risk and analytics stack, with positions and exposures reconciled overnight through BlackRock Aladdin and market data sourced from Bloomberg. This infrastructure lets us stress-test allocations against scenarios spanning a 250 basis point rate shock and a sharp widening in credit spreads.

## Regional Themes

In the United States, large-cap technology continues to lead, and we retain a position in Apple ($AAPL), ISIN US0378331005, as a core holding within the growth sleeve. Emerging markets look attractive on valuation, though selectivity is essential given the divergence in fiscal trajectories. Across our flagship strategy we have committed roughly USD 1.2 billion to opportunities we believe are mispriced relative to fundamentals.

## Risks to the View

The principal risk to our constructive stance is a reacceleration of inflation that forces central banks to halt or reverse the easing cycle. A disorderly repricing in rates would weigh on both equities and fixed income simultaneously, eroding the diversification benefit that anchors most balanced portfolios.
