# Fixed Income Commentary: Navigating the Easing Cycle

## Market Context

Bond markets have repriced sharply over the past quarter as investors recalibrated expectations for the pace of monetary easing. With central banks signaling a cumulative 250 basis points of cuts ahead, the front end of the curve has rallied while the long end has lagged, leaving the yield curve materially steeper than it began the year.

## Issuance and Demand

Primary markets have been remarkably active. In the first quarter alone, one billion dollars of new investment grade supply was absorbed comfortably by a demand base hungry for duration ahead of the easing cycle. We expect issuance to remain elevated through Q2 2026 as corporate treasurers lock in financing before any reacceleration in rates.

## Sector Views

Within credit we maintain a preference for investment grade over high yield. Spreads at the riskier end of the market have compressed to 3.5% over comparable government bonds, a level that we judge offers insufficient compensation for the deterioration we expect in default rates later in the cycle. We are adding selectively to high-quality financial issuers.

## Portfolio Construction

Duration positioning across the strategy is modestly long the benchmark, expressed primarily through the five-to-ten-year segment of the curve. Risk and scenario analysis runs through BlackRock Aladdin, with intraday pricing and liquidity metrics drawn from Bloomberg. The aggregate strategy manages close to USD 1.2 billion across government and corporate mandates.

## Emerging Market Debt

Hard-currency emerging market sovereigns look attractive on a selective basis, particularly issuers with credible fiscal paths and improving external balances. We have rotated a portion of the portfolio toward these names while hedging the residual currency exposure where cost permits.

## Outlook

The base case remains constructive for high-quality fixed income. A gradual easing cycle, contained inflation near 3.5%, and a steepening curve together create a supportive environment for carry and modest capital appreciation over the balance of the year.

Forecasts are not a reliable indicator of future performance and are based on assumptions that may not materialize. Currency movements may increase or decrease returns for investors.
