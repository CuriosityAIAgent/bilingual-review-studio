# European Equity Strategy: Positioning for the Recovery

## Overview

European equities have lagged their US counterparts for much of the past decade, but the relative valuation gap has widened to a point we consider compelling. With the regional economy stabilizing and earnings revisions turning positive, we see scope for a catch-up trade over the course of 2026.

## Earnings and Valuations

Consensus expects corporate earnings across the index to grow 3.5% this year, a modest figure that we believe is conservative. Margins have held up better than feared, and several cyclical sectors are entering the year with lean inventories and improving order books. At current multiples, the market is pricing in little of this potential upside.

## Sector Positioning

We are overweight industrials and financials, and underweight consumer staples. The automotive sector merits particular attention: as electrification accelerates, the average car sold in Europe now carries far more semiconductor content than a decade ago, reshaping supplier economics and creating winners and losers along the value chain. We favor component suppliers with pricing power over the assemblers themselves.

## Risk Management

Position sizing across the strategy is governed through BlackRock Aladdin, with daily exposure reports cross-checked against Bloomberg pricing. Our largest single-name active weight is capped, and we run the book against a 250 basis point parallel rate shock to ensure the portfolio can withstand a renewed tightening in financial conditions. Total assets in the strategy stand at roughly USD 1.2 billion.

## Currency Considerations

For unhedged investors, currency movements will be a meaningful driver of returns. A stronger euro would amplify local-currency gains for dollar-based investors, while a weaker euro would erode them. We currently run a partial hedge and will adjust dynamically as the rate differential evolves through Q2 2026.

## Conclusion

We believe European equities offer an attractive entry point for patient, valuation-sensitive investors. The combination of depressed sentiment, reasonable multiples, and improving fundamentals is the kind of setup that has historically rewarded a contrarian stance.
